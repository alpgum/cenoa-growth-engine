## Executive Summary

Over the 12-week period, Cenoa saw a dramatic **install surge peaking at W7 (Feb 10–16) with 8,447 installs** — 8.4× the W1 baseline — driven primarily by Google Search campaigns in Nigeria and Egypt. Since then, installs have fallen sharply to 1,092 in W12, while **new actives remained remarkably stable (831–1,101/week)**, suggesting the install surge brought low-quality traffic that didn't convert to activated users.

**Key finding:** The funnel efficiency (install → new active) is *inversely correlated* with install volume — higher installs dilute conversion quality.

## Notable Inflection Points

1. **W3 (Jan 13):** First major install jump (+90.4% WoW). "Other" paid channel surged from 249 → 1,306. Nigeria installs tripled.

2. **W4 (Jan 20):** Google Search turned on aggressively (74 → 1,242). KYC submissions appeared for the first time (81). This week marks the start of the major paid push.

3. **W7 (Feb 10):** **Peak week.** 8,447 installs. Google Search at 5,823. NG and EG at all-time highs. But new actives actually *dipped* to 910 — the worst Install→NA rate (10.8%).

4. **W8 (Feb 17):** Google Search cut by ~80% (5,823 → 1,200). Appnext emerged as replacement (607). Interestingly, new actives *increased* to 1,044 — best level in weeks.

5. **W9 (Feb 24):** Google Search essentially stopped (54). Appnext peaked (1,308). TR installs spiked to 1,950. Channel mix fundamentally shifted.

6. **W10 (Mar 3):** Virtual accounts hit 751 (2× normal) — delayed conversion from W7 surge filtering through. New actives peaked at 1,101.

7. **W12 (Mar 17):** Return to near-baseline. All channels at minimal levels. Appnext collapsed (708 → 1). Signup/install rate at 92.9% — highest in 12 weeks, confirming organic traffic converts best.

## Strategic Implications

1. **Egypt is not converting.** Despite thousands of installs, new actives from EG averaged <10/week. The EG market needs either different targeting or deprioritization.

2. **Nigeria paid quality is poor.** NG installs varied 10× but new actives barely moved. The organic NG base (~250–300 new actives/week) is stable regardless of paid spend.

3. **Turkey is the reliable market.** TR new actives are stable and respond slightly to paid efforts. TR should be the primary target for performance marketing.

4. **Google Search at scale produced low-quality installs.** The W7 peak of 5,823 Google Search installs produced no measurable lift in new actives. Budget was likely wasted on broad targeting in NG/EG.

5. **Organic baseline is strong.** ~600–900 installs/week organically, with 70–90% signup rates. These users are the most valuable.

6. **KYC ramp is promising.** Steady growth from 0 → 125/week suggests the new KYC flow is gaining traction.
